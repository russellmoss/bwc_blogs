>
> — **Mike Juergens** | Source: **BWG**

>

## 2. Bhutan Climate & Microclimates

> "I have a tremendous hedge against climate change because I have literally every climate zone. So if the world continues to heat up like a crockpot set on low — 50 years from now, Champagne's done. Bordeaux's done. You know what I do? I drive 15 miles north and my average temperature goes down 10 degrees."
>
> — **Mike Juergens** | Source: **BWG**

> "The environment lends itself well to this plethora of microclimates. Which I think leads us to believe that there's some real opportunities to grow some amazing wine. We just gotta find what goes well in what pocket."
>
> — **Mike Juergens** | Source: **BWG**

> "At the bottom of the country, the south side is about 500 feet in elevation, and it is jungle — monkeys, elephants, tigers. Then you move up into the 3,000 to 8,000–10,000 foot range, which is where a lot of the main cities are. And then the further north you go, the highest point is 27,000 feet. The highest unclimbed mountain on the planet is in Bhutan."
>
> — **Mike Juergens** | Source: **BWG**

> "Nobody knows what grapes are gonna grow in Bhutan. The altitude ranges from 500 feet in the south to 27,000 feet in the north. So no one knows where the right spot is to grow. It's never been done before."
>
> — **Mike Juergens** | Source: **BBB**

> "I have vineyards planted at altitudes from 2,000 feet up to 9,000 feet, and I have red grapes and white grapes and all kinds of different stuff growing. I am guaranteed the only person in the world that has vineyards where Riesling and Cabernet Sauvignon are planted next to each other."
>
> — **Mike Juergens** | Source: **BBB**

> "The country goes from about 500 feet in altitude on the south side to 27,000 feet. And in a very small area — it's like the size of Switzerland. So a couple hundred miles, you just keep going up."
>
> — **Mike Juergens** | Source: **JCB**

> "The climate range is there. We can grow in some really hot climates that are a hundred degrees. And we can grow in some real cool climates that are 70. The cooler climate stuff in particular, we're gonna have a lot of restrained fruit and more of that minerality of the region."
>
> — **Mike Juergens** | Source: **JCB**

> "At the base of the country it's 500 feet in elevation. At the north it's 25,000 feet. So it just basically goes straight up. Depending on where you are, you have different things."
>
> — **Mike Juergens** | Source: **B8F**

> "If it gets two degrees warmer in Bordeaux or Champagne or Napa, it's a huge problem. If it gets two degrees warmer in the Himalayas, I just go up a thousand feet — that's 10 miles down the street. I'm hedged. When I step back and think about what the next thousand years might look like for the wine regions of the world, I don't know that Napa's in business for the next thousand years. But the Himalayas — maybe."
>
> — **Mike Juergens** | Source: **B8F**

> "The south side is only about 500 feet. The north side is about 27,000 feet at the highest point, and that span is only like 400 miles. So in a very short space, you have every climate zone."
>
> — **Mike Juergens** | Source: **FA**

> "As climate change is impacting traditional wine zones — like Bordeaux — if it warms up for us, we just move up a little bit and we're hedged against it."
>
> — **Mike Juergens** | Source: **FA**

> "Bhutan on the south side is about 500 feet in elevation, and on the north side it's about 27,000 feet. That's 300 to 400 miles north to south. So you have every climate zone known to man in one little country the size of Switzerland."
>
> — **Mike Juergens** | Source: **CDV**

> "The vast diversity of Bhutanese microclimates is what makes this project spectacular, but it also means that we have to approach each vineyard individually in terms of figuring out what to plant, how to plant it, how to farm it."
>
> — **Mike Juergens** | Source: **WE**

> "You can go south, north or up."
>
> — **Mike Juergens**, on climate change adaptation | Source: **WE**

## 3. Bhutan Soil

> "I think it's gonna be pretty savory. There's a lot of minerality that's gonna come. The soil there is so diverse — it's granitic, it's alluvial runoff from the glaciers, it's clay, it's iron. Really diverse soil."
>
> — **Mike Juergens** | Source: **JCB**

> "If you take a scoop of Bhutanese soil and you look at it in your hand, there's just so many microorganisms in it. It's teeming with life. Four different colors of dirt and six different worms and two little bugs you don't know what they are."
>
> — **Mike Juergens** | Source: **JCB**

> "The soil is pretty varied. Whatever tectonic forces pushed the Himalayas out — you have granite, you have sand, you have clay, you have these big iron ridges that almost look like terra rossa. And then you get alluvial runoff because the glaciers are eroding stuff and the water's carrying it down. And you'll have all of those within the same vineyard."
>
> — **Mike Juergens** | Source: **CDV**

> "Even within a single field, you'll see 10 different kinds of soil just because of the way the Himalayas were formed."
>
> — **Mike Juergens** | Source: **CDV**

> "The water table is above you and it's in the form of glaciers."
>
> — **Mike Juergens** | Source: **CDV**

## 7. Reasons to Believe in Bhutan Wine Company

> "We got another 1,920 acres of vines to plant in Bhutan. We're gonna make that country relevant on the global wine scene. And it will express a sense of place, hopefully in a really positive way."
>
> — **Mike Juergens** | Source: **BWG**

> "If Marco Polo had detoured through Bhutan when he was cruising the Silk Road with some grapes, these guys would be making some kick-ass wine."
>
> — **Mike Juergens** | Source: **BWG**

> "You get one ride on the merry-go-round. Everyone says find stuff that you're passionate about and do that, and then you have an amazing life. Everything that I do — I only wanna do epic shit with cool people. That's all I want to do. But if it's that, I'm all in."
>
> — **Mike Juergens** | Source: **BWG**

> "Most places that are making wine have had a couple hundred or a couple thousand years to figure out how to dial it in. We're gonna learn. We're gonna make mistakes. >
> — **Mike Juergens** | Source: **BBB**

> "I know with 100% certainty that a bunch of that stuff is not gonna work. And I'm totally fine with that. I don't need everything to work. I need to find the one thing that does work. If that ends up being Malbec and Bhutan becomes the Malbec capital of the world — well, that's what it is."
>
> — **Mike Juergens** | Source: **BBB**
